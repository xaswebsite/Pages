Aiming For The Stars-Women's Day

On the 25th of March 2023, the women of the Xaverian Astronomical Society (XAS) in collaboration with the Post Graduate and Research Department of Physics, organized a science-cum-astronomy awareness session, “Aiming for the Stars”, for the underprivileged girls of our society under the guidance of the National Service Scheme (NSS). For our venture, we went to the Calcutta Muslim Orphanage located at Ripon Street to teach the girls there that Science is nothing to be scared of and to encourage them to think scientifically and maybe select Science as their field of study in the future.
We had stayed back in college the day before the event making charts on science and women in science. On the day, when we reached the orphanage, we were greeted by many girls from classes 8 to 10 who were eager to learn about science.
The event started off with us introducing ourselves and asking the girls to introduce themselves too. Most girls said they had plans of taking Bengali, Geography, or English as their subject of study in the future. Only one girl replied that she liked studying Life Science. We showed them the various charts about science and various women who had to overcome many adversities to become successful in science. They were fascinated to learn about the many Indian women in STEM such as Kalpana Chawla and Anandibai Gopalrao Joshi who had excelled in their fields.
The girls had many questions for us such as why we decided to study science, why people weigh lesser on the moon, and how they should excel in their science classes without being able to afford tuition classes.
Then we divided them into groups so that we could show them presentations that we had prepared on our laptops and to facilitate one-to-one interaction between the girls and us. The girls listened in awe as we explained to them how the phases of the moon change, showed them pictures of Venus' occultation that had taken place a day before and told them the story of Marie Curie's struggles at the end of which she became the winner of two Nobel prizes.
The event ended with one girl from each group telling us about what they learned and what they thought of our presentations. We also handed out leaflets with information regarding the various scholarships they can avail of for their further studies in science.

The visit provided us an insight into the lives of these underprivileged girls who despite their circumstances are filled with a passion for learning which made them so eager to listen to what we had to say. The event was also very emotionally fulfilling for us students as we all left with the feeling that we had done some good work and had been able to help some people. The event highlighted the importance of supporting institutions providing a haven for vulnerable children. It also reinforced the fact that it is not that Indian girls lack interest in science but that like every person they just need someone to relate to and tell them that it may be difficult but never impossible nor boring to decide to study science.
